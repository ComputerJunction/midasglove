How can I help you?

The purpose of this website is to help investors and researchers that use SEC data. How can you make a better decision? You gather more information, more numbers, and then you analyze those numbers. BOOM! Decision Making Simplified!

Of Course I'm joking. It is not that simple. So What can you expect in this website? You can expect accurate data that can be used for analysis. You can expect simplified data that can be used to create models. So what you can expect is the building blocks for creating something.

The focus is on institutional investment managers, the Whales, the Big Money firms that buy and sell and move the markets. These market players have a huge influence on a stock you own.

SEC classification of institutional investment managers. Simply: They have at least $100M available for investment.

I live in the world of questions so I will write this introduction through the lens of questions. The data on the website should answer questions. So ask some questions?

When is a good time to buy a stock? When the price goes down, of course BUT (which negates the previous statement) this is true if you think the share price will increase in the future.

When will the price increase? When more buyers request purchase of the security then there are sellers but you don't always need a large number of buyers you could settle for one buyer that requests an outsided large number of shares of a single security.

How would I know if a Whale wants a specific stock? Look at their past purchases, get in the head of their decision makers, ask them what they will do next.

Trying to predict others behavior is fun activity if you don't mind being incorrect a majority of the time. "Smart money" (professionals? people that have a lot to lose if they make bad decisions) worries more the return and the error rate; really they worry about their relationship.

I told you before this website focuses on the institutional investment managers, that is because they are the "smart money" and love them or hate them they can make the market move with their decisions.

Probably an intelligent move to at least know what have done, and have some inkling about what they might do in the future.

How can you be more like smart money? Use all the available to data to make good decisions.

Thankfully I am here to help. I will give you data. You make the good decision.

If you want to know about the Treasury Bond Yield Curve the New York Federal Reserve is here to help.

Okay show me something...

Curious? Good, stay curious it is a great quality.

First step: Click one of the links at the top of the page. Each page will provide a list that can be filtered down providing more granular details about that subject.

I structured the website in a step-ladder fashion, you will be presented with a large, macro, set of information and then you can filter that information down to some actionable set of information.

Example: You are wondering about an institutional investor so you click on the Institutional Investor List.

Steps:

  1. You click on the Institutional Investor List
  2. You search for your interesting Instituional Investor
  3. This click will show you their most recent security holdings based on their most recent Form 13K filings
  4. From this point you can:
  5. You can filter their holdings
  6. You can view their past holdings
  7. You can see a graph representation of their holdings to see the changes from one time period to another time period

Example 2: You are wondering about a security - Apple has multiple securities - Apple for Example

Steps:

  1. You click on the Central Index Key, Cusip, Ticker link
  2. You search for your company name, cusip number, ticker symbol
  3. You click on your interested company
  4. The next page shows all the possible securities provided by that company. Oh La La so many choices
  5. You click on one of the selections
  6. You will be presented with the list of institutional investors can currently holding that security

Have you ever wondered if the farming industry relies on the health care industry? Have you thought who does IBM pay in order to produce those great products?

If you have never asked that question, now it is in your head. So you are welcome. For those that have pondered these questions I think I have some data for you.

I decided to use the US Bureau of Economic Analysis to ask this question because I am a proud American. 'MERICA 'MERICA!!!! and...

They track US industries and most the institutional investors tracked by the SEC are US companies so... moving on

The Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) provides some really cool data, they provide these dryly (my opinion is valid) labeled "Input - Output" tables.

These tables come in many versions, and the version that I incorporate on this website is the requirements input output table.

This table shows the portion of a dollar spent by an industry on a specific commodity.

I know at this point you might say so what?

I think this is really interesting because these tables give an actual numerical weight (in this case it may be better to say %) that can be used to figure out the whole dollar amount of revenue a specific company spends buying commodities/services to produce their product/service.

Question time again $ Would it be useful to know if one industry is heavily dependent on a specific commodity/service in order to produce its own product?

What happens when that commodity become scarce or plentiful?

What should I see in the companies financials in those situations?
